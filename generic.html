Alene Wecker

Bio

Hailed as “a true Isolde” by Heldentenor Jon Frederic West, Alene Wecker flourishes in the dramatic repertory of Wagner, Verdi, Strauss, and Puccini. Her voice has been praised for its “freakish” fullness in the lower register, coupled with an easy spin in the top. The size, color, and range of her voice lend themselves easily to much of the most demanding repertoire for dramatic sopranos, as she sings both Isolde and Turandot with beauty and ease.

2018 has been a busy but promising year for Ms. Wecker. In October, she will join Éva Marton in an intensive role study of the titled role in Turandot, followed by a concert performance of the work at the Franz Liszt Academy in Budapest, Hungary. In August, she performed Gertrude in Hansel und Gretel with the Victoria/Vancouver Coaching Experience, and in June, she made her Wagner debut with Resonanz Opera, singing Isolde in Act II of Tristan und Isolde.

In prior seasons, Ms. Wecker sang such roles as the Witch in Hansel and Gretel, Mercédès in Carmen, La Badessa in Suor Angelica, and Olga Olsen in Street Scene. She has also sung much of the comedic Gilbert and Sullivan mezzo-soprano/contralto roles, including both Ruth and Kate from Pirates of Penzance, Dame Carruthers from The Yeoman of the Guard, Tessa from The Gondoliers, and Katisha from The Mikado.

Ms. Wecker has been a featured soloist in over 30 concerts, including repertoire such as Rossini’s Stabat Mater, Bach’s Magnificat, Vivaldi’s Stabat Mater, and Vaughan Williams’ Serenade to Music. Along with Verdi and Strauss arias, art songs such as Barber’s Hermit Songs, Schumann’s Frauenliebe und Leben, De Falla’s Siete canciones populares espanoles (with guitar), and Britten’s A Charm of Lullabies, frequently comprise her concert choices. She has also collaborated with local composers in premiering their works. (M. Ryan Taylor, Nelson Leduc, Joey Calkins).

Though most comfortable in the dramatic soprano repertory, Ms. Wecker continues to sing second alto as a professional chorister with Utah Opera (since 2011); her easy low notes giving strength to the ensemble. She also sings occasionally with the Utah Symphony. The American born and raised soprano has spent extended time in Romania, and is conversant in both Romanian and German.
