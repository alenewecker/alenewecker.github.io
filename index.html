About Me

With a voice described as “chocolate and chili powder”, Ms. Wecker’s soprano voice radiates warmth and dramatic cut. As a performer in the greater Salt Lake area since 2004, Ms. Wecker made her debut solo performance in Salt Lake’s Capitol Theater in Utah Opera’s AriaFest 2017, singing Leonora’s opening aria and cabaletta from Il trovatore (Verdi). In the summer of 2017, she was one of three Americans admitted into the Lotte Lehmann Akademie, where she was a featured soloist in the German towns of Perleberg, Bad Wilsnack, and Glövzin, singing Lady Macbeth’s aria “Vieni t’affretta” and “My Man’s Gone Now” from Gershwin’s Porgy and Bess. Though she likes the challenge of both new and obscure works, Ms. Wecker feels most at home in the works of Verdi and Strauss.

Ms Wecker began the switch to soprano repertoire in 2015, before which she sang such iconic mezzo roles as the Witch in Hansel and Gretel, Mercedes in Carmen, and Ruth in Pirates of Penzance (to name a few). Described as a Verdi soprano by Maestro Nicholas Giusti, Ms. Wecker has studied and performed arias from the roles of Amelia in Un ballo in maschera, Elena in I vespri Siciliani, Lady MacBeth, and Leonora in Il trovatore. Currently, she is exploring the Strauss repertoire of Elektra, and Ariadne, and hopes to have the time to try out some Wagner very soon. But her favorite piece to sing right now is “Inflammatus et accensus” from Rossini’s Stabat Mater, because that number is smokin’!

Currently a Master’s student at Brigham Young University, she studies with Dr. Diane Reich. Ms Wecker is proficient in German and Romanian, though she rarely has occasion to speak the latter. In the summer of 2017, Ms. Wecker organized a production of Menotti’s the Consul as a benefit concert for Utah refugees. Though grateful to be able to present such a poignant opera, directly applicable to the crises of our day, Ms Wecker will gladly perform any role on stage before she again accepts the role of producer!
